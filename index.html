KitchenBoss: a new way for co-op kitchen managers to get stuff done

Jack Reidy
Engineer at KitchenBoss

Published May 17, 2018

Behind every happy and healthy co-op is a kitchen manager stocking the house with fresh and delicious food. Grocery shopping for an entire community of students with diverse taste preferences and dietary restrictions takes a lot of time and care.

As hard as the work is, there aren’t great tools to keep kitchens running smoothly. Juggling email threads, shared documents, whiteboards, and surveys just to nail down an order is tedious and frustrating. There isn’t an app for the tasks most important to kitchen managers, and they really deserve better tools to get the job done. That’s why we built KitchenBoss, a simple, powerful service that helps you manage inventory and create shopping lists.

Manage Inventory

KitchenBoss is like a shopping cart, spreadsheet, and to-do list simplified into one intuitive interface. We’re prioritizing organization because kitchen managers have to keep track of so many different items. The app arranges your food products in “tabs” which correspond to different self-defined categories. Tabs are designed for flexibility so that you can organize your inventory checklist according to your process. If you’re making a Costco order, simply pull up the “Costco” tab so that you only see items relevant to your inventory search.

Create Shopping Lists

Laptops are cumbersome companions when you’re checking inventory. That’s why KitchenBoss is designed to work with you in your kitchen. The mobile app gives you a free hand as you search for all the items on your list. You won’t need to worry about keeping your laptop safe, clean, and out of the way because it’ll be safe in your room. Instead, take the KitchenBoss app the next time you dive headfirst into the back of the produce bin.

KitchenBoss also handles the “gotchas” of creating a new shopping list. When enabled, an order assistant recommends items based on previous orders. KitchenBoss will let you know if you’re ordering too many pickles and not enough sandwich bread.

Take Requests

One of the most common complaints we heard from kitchen managers is how hard it can be to satisfy every appetite when requests arrive at all hours of the day from a wide variety of communication channels. That’s why KitchenBoss is designed to make feedback easy.

Residents can submit requests to your KitchenBoss account without downloading anything. Share links to your request page over email, SMS, or anywhere else you communicate with the house. KitchenBoss unifies incoming messages so that you don’t have to check on all of your accounts. The next time a resident asks about peanut butter pretzels while you’re trying to study for finals, point them to your request page.
